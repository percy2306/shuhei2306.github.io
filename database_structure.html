Overview

The above (Figure 1) shows the overview of the AIST Dance DB. This database consists of 1,618 street dances in 13,939 videos including

13,890 videos of 10 street dance genres,
Forty-nine additional videos with three different situations.

The following table shows a summary of the dances and the number of videos.

Situation

Each of 10 dance genres has 1,389 videos that consist of 4 categories:

Basic Dance (120 dances in 1,080 videos for basic genre-specific dance moves with four impressions (ChoreoType): intense, loose, hard, and soft),
Advanced Dance (21 dances in 189 videos for advanced dance moves originally choreographed by each individual dancer),
Group Dance (10 dances in 90 videos for group dance done with three dancers having different original choreographies),
Moving Camera (10 dances in 30 dolly-shot videos with moving camera).

Basic Dance

1,080 videos per genre (= 3 dancers * 10 dances * 4 variations * 9 cameras)


Advanced Dance

189 videos per genre (= 3 dancers * 7 dances * 9 cameras + 1 dance)
Advanced dance set consists of 12 dances choreographed by themselves.

Group Dance

90 videos per genre (= 1 group * 10 dances * 9 cameras)
a group consists of 3 dancers performs 10 dances choreographed by themselves

Moving Camera

30 videos per genre (= 10 dances * 3 cameras)

Additional Videos

Showcase (3 dances in 24 videos assuming stage dance performances by 10 dancers in front of audiences)
Cypher (2 dances in 10 videos where 10 dancers line up in a circle and keep dancing in turns)
Battle (3 dances in 15 videos where 2 dancers face each other and dance)

Showcase

27 videos
96 beats, 75 seconds (average)
3 dances by 10 dancers

Cypher

10 videos
10 dancers line up in a circle and keep turns to dance in the center for approximately 1 minute.
Dancers improvised the dances on the music played at the moment.
The timing to end the dance was up to the dancer.
Music clips were newly created for this occasion.
The duration of a set of dances was 10 minutes, and 2 sets were filmed in total.

Battle

15 videos
Two dancers create a group and 3 groups in total take turns to dance in front of 5 cameras.
Two dancers in a group face each other and dancers perform a dance one by one for approximately 1 minute each.
The timing of the change of the dancer was up to the dancers.
Music clips were newly created for this occasion.
TA set lasted 4 minutes and 3 sets were filmed in total.

Dance Genre

The dance genres are the Old School styles, which are dance styles from the 1970s to 1990s, and the New School styles, which are dance styles since the 1990s. We have asked the experts on street dances to set the dance genres.

Dancer

To cover choreographic variations within dance genres, we had 40 professional dancers (25 male and 15 female) participated in video recordings in a professional studio. At least three dancers performed each genre. All dancers had more than five years of dance experience. All videos were recorded in full color, although dancers mainly wore monotone clothing when dancing. Please see [Dance Info] for more detailed information of the dancers.

Camera

We have fixed all camera positions except for the moving camera. The hight of the cameras was 180 cm above the floor except for camera C09 which was 40 cm above the floor. The location of the front camera was carefully designed to capture the full body of the dancer, and the other cameras were located to capture as much of the dancer’s body as possible except for group dance.

Choreography

You can download the list of BasicDances from [here]. The names of dances are described at [Database Download].

Regarding dances other than BasicDances, Dancers were asked to choreograph their dance to fit the given genre. Each choreography was filmed in one take; however, it was retaken where there was an apparent mistake.

Music

This database includes 60 musical pieces in ten dance genres. Each musical piece is available for download at [Get Only Music Data].

The tempi of the six pieces (one piece for one genre, except for House) were set to 80, 90, 100, 110, 120, and 130 beats per minute (BPM). The tempi for the six pieces of House were set to 110, 115, 120, 125, 130, and 135 BPM.
